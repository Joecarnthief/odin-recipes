Egg Drop Soup

Hardware

  • A medium soup pot
  • spoon
  • small bowl
  • hands or other manipulators

Software

  • 1 Liter Chicken Stock
  • 1/2 Teaspoon sesame oil
  • 3/4 Teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 Teaspoon white pepper
  • 3 Tablespoons cornstarch mixed with 1/3 cup of cold water
  • 3-4 Eggs
  • 1-2 Scallions
  • (Optional) 1/4 Teaspoon MSG

Program

  1. Bring the chicken stock to a simmer in a medium soup pot. Stir in the sesame oil, salt, sugar, white pepper, and MSG (if using).
  2. Next add the cornstarch and water mixture. Make sure the cornstarch and water is mixed well, as the cornstarch settles very quickly. Stir the soup continuously as you drizzle in the slurry, or you'll get clumps of cooked starch in your soup. Use more or less starch if you like a thicker or thinner soup. You can also add the starch in a couple small batches, let the soup simmer for a couple of minutes, then check to see if the consistency is to your liking.
  3. This recipe calls for the egg to be lightly beaten, which results in both white and yellow egg swirls. The speed at which you stir the soup when adding the egg also determines whether you get large “egg flowers” or small egg flowers (i.e. swirly bits of egg). Use a ladle to stir the soup in a circular motion, and slowly drizzle in the egg until you have added it all.
  4. Ladle the soup into bowls, top with scallions, and serve!
